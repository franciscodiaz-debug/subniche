# Design and Tone

## Product feel

SubNiche should feel:

- expert-friendly,
- clean,
- confident,
- understated,
- community-native in spirit,
- premium without being precious,
- intuitive for serious casual users.

It should not feel like:

- a generic classifieds site,
- a corporate SaaS dashboard,
- a crypto marketplace,
- a social network chasing engagement for its own sake,
- too complex for casual users.

## Voice

Use language that is:

- direct,
- calm,
- knowledgeable,
- nonchalant,
- specific.

Avoid language that is:

- breathless,
- generic,
- corporate,
- over-explained,
- hype-driven.

## Preferred phrases

Good:

- "A smarter marketplace, for those who know"
- "Find your people"
- "More than just a listing"
- "Trust is built in"
- "Trading made easy"
- "Trading is native"

Avoid:

- "Unlock the power of community commerce"
- "First-class trading behavior"
- "Seamless marketplace ecosystem"
- "AI-powered social commerce platform"

## Listing cards

Listing cards should emphasize:

- strong photo,
- title,
- price or trade value/status,
- small trade/swap icon when also open to trade,
- key attributes,
- approximate location/distance,
- seller/profile trust signals where space allows.

Do not overload cards with every attribute.
Do not crowd listing cards with fulfillment details; fulfillment belongs on item detail page and filters.

## Item view page

There is one core item view page. Sale, trade, collection, wishlist, owner, and permission elements are additive modules based on item state and viewer access.

Do not create fully separate mental models for collection item page vs listing page unless implementation routing requires it internally.

## Collections

Collections should feel like identity and context, not unpublished listings.

A collection preview may show:

- owner/collector identity,
- strong item thumbnails,
- collection title,
- item count,
- useful trust/expertise context,
- owner notes where supported.

Collection comments are not MVP.

## Empty states

Empty states should be helpful and understated.

Good:

- "List your first item"
- "Add something to your collection"
- "Set what you'd trade for"
- "Add a wishlist item"

Bad:

- "Nothing here yet!!!"
- "Be the first to revolutionize this marketplace"
- "Unlock your selling potential"

## AI listing assist

AI should feel helpful and quiet.

Frame AI as reducing drudge work, not replacing authenticity.

AI should not feel like the main product.

## Trade language

Use:

- 2-way match,
- 1-way match,
- criteria-complete,
- Possible Match,
- trade interests.

Avoid:

- True Match,
- Full Match,
- Wanted Item.
