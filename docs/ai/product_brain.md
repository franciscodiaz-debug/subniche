# SubNiche Product Brain

## One-sentence description

SubNiche is a niche-first person-to-person marketplace where enthusiasts can buy, sell, trade, organize collections, signal what they are looking for, and present niche-specific identities.

## Core idea

Generic marketplaces are noisy. Niche communities have trust, identity, expertise, and shared language.

SubNiche uses niche identity to create a better marketplace experience. The product should feel like walking into the right room with people who understand the gear, not browsing a digital yard sale.

## Primary users

### Enthusiasts

People who know a niche deeply, care about details, and value interacting with other people who understand the same category.

Examples:

- guitar players and collectors,
- motorcycle and powersports gear people,
- coin collectors,
- audiophiles,
- car enthusiasts,
- other category-specific communities later.

### Serious casual users

People who are not experts yet but want a better marketplace than Facebook Marketplace, Craigslist, or generic classified sites.

### Product-owner / super-admin

For MVP, SubNiche requires an internal product-owner/super-admin role that can configure niches, categories, subcategories, attributes, allowed values, copy, and imagery.

Community-owner tools are post-MVP.

## Differentiators

### Niche-first identity

The niche is the home context, not just a filter. Items, listings, collections, wishlist items, and profiles live within a niche.

### Trading is native

Users can list items for trade and define what they would accept through structured trade interests. Trade matching is criteria-driven and built into the product.

### Collections create single-player value

Users can organize and show items even when they are not selling. Collections create identity, context, and a reason to participate before transacting.

### Wishlist items create demand signals

Wishlist items let users show what they are looking for. They are not trade interests and do not unlock formal offers in MVP, but they help other users discover demand and start conversations.

### Structured taxonomy powers discovery

Niche-specific categories, subcategories, and attributes make listings, filters, wishlist items, search, and trade matching better.

## Product principles

- Preserve niche identity.
- Make listing easier.
- Make trading understandable.
- Make profiles and collections matter.
- Avoid generic marketplace noise.
- Reward quality over volume.
- Let collections act as identity/context.
- Keep the MVP focused.
- Do not overbuild transaction infrastructure before core listing/trading value is proven.

## Anti-goals

SubNiche should not become:

- a generic classifieds board,
- a Facebook Marketplace clone,
- a pure transaction-processing marketplace,
- a social feed with commerce bolted on,
- a crypto/web3-style marketplace,
- an overbuilt marketplace before core listing/trading value is clear.

## MVP product shape

MVP should prove the core loop:

1. Choose or enter a niche.
2. Add an item, listing, collection, or wishlist item.
3. Browse active marketplace listings within that niche.
4. Set trade interests for for-trade listings.
5. See criteria-driven 2-way and 1-way trade opportunities.
6. Message users in item/listing/wishlist/collection-item context.
7. Send simple cash offers or trade offers where allowed.
8. Maintain niche-specific profile context.

Communities, discussions, comments, public reputation, payment processing, and fulfillment infrastructure come later.
